"Exploring More Effective Speech Representation via ASR for High Quality of End-to-End Multi-Speaker TTS"


Authors: Dawei Liu, Longbiao Wang, Sheng Li, Haoyu Li, Chenchen Ding, Ju Zhang and Jianwu Dang

Abstract: The quality of multi-speaker Text-to-Speech (TTS) is composed of speech naturalness and speaker similarity. The current multi-speaker TTS based on speaker embedding extracted by Speaker Verification (SV) or Speaker Recognition (SR) model has made significant progress in the speaker similarity of synthesized speech. However, SV/SR tasks build speaker space based on the differences between speakers in the training set, the speaker embedding extracted by them can improve the speaker similarity but lacking a positive influence on naturalness. Unlike SV/SR-based systems, the ASR encoder outputs contain relatively complete speech information, such as speaker information, timbre, prosody. Therefore, we propose using the ASR encoder to extract speech embedding to improve multi-speaker TTS quality, especially for speech naturalness. To enable the ASR system to learn the speaker characteristics better, we explicitly feed the speaker-id to the training label. The experimental results show that the speech embedding extracted by proposed methods has good speaker characteristics and has beneficial acoustic information for speech naturalness. The proposed method significantly improves the naturalness and similarity of multi-speaker TTS.
